# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define and execute quality assurance strategies. They own test coverage, acceptance validation, and quality gates for releases. They work closely with developers and product managers to ensure features meet quality standards before production release.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy, scope, and coverage requirements
- Create and maintain comprehensive test plans and checklists
- Execute manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria before feature sign-off
- Identify quality risks and propose mitigations
- Support release readiness verification and smoke tests
- Track defects and regressions

### Goals
- Ensure customer-ready quality before release
- Reduce production defects through comprehensive testing
- Improve test automation and efficiency
- Enable faster iteration through clear quality gates
- Build confidence in product quality across the organization

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Collaborate with **Developers** on testability requirements and test automation frameworks
- Work with **Product Managers** to clarify and validate acceptance criteria
- Coordinate with **Project Managers** on testing timelines, resource planning, and quality gates
- Report quality metrics and risks to **Stakeholders** and escalate blocking quality issues
- Support **Technical Leads** on integration testing, performance testing, and architectural concerns
- Partner with **Security/Compliance Officer** on security testing and vulnerability assessment

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement (test requirements)
- Pre-release quality sign-offs and release notes
- Defect reports, regression test results, and quality dashboards
- Weekly quality metrics and risk register updates
- Test strategy presentations for complex features

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, make architectural decisions, and guide the team on technical best practices and risk mitigation. They ensure the system is designed for scalability, maintainability, and performance.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Make or facilitate key technical decisions
- Review code architecture and design for quality
- Identify technical risks and propose solutions
- Guide technology choices and tool evaluation
- Mentor and coach the team on technical excellence
- Participate in design reviews and technical spikes

### Goals
- Ensure scalable, maintainable technical design
- Reduce technical debt and future rework
- Enable team velocity through clear technical direction
- Proactively identify and mitigate technical risks
- Foster a culture of technical excellence

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Guide **Developers** on design decisions, architectural patterns, and best practices
- Collaborate with **Product Managers** on feasibility analysis and technical trade-offs
- Work with **Project Managers** on resource planning for complex, high-risk features
- Partner with **QA/Testing Lead** on performance testing, integration testing approaches, and testability
- Report technical risks and dependencies to **Stakeholders** and escalate blockers
- Coordinate with **Security/Compliance Officer** on secure design practices and threat modeling
- Work with **Scrum Master** on technical impediments and process blockers

### Typical Communication
- Technical design reviews and architecture discussions
- Code review feedback, architecture mentoring, and design guidance
- Risk register updates on technical and architectural risks
- Sprint planning and estimation sessions (technical complexity)
- Technical spike results and proof-of-concept outcomes
- Technology evaluation and tool selection recommendations

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors represent business interests, approve budgets and scope, and provide strategic direction and authority to remove organizational and business blockers. They are invested in project outcomes and success metrics.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business context and strategic priorities
- Approve project budget, scope, and timeline
- Remove organizational, business, and policy blockers
- Validate business outcomes and return on investment (ROI)
- Provide decision authority when needed
- Communicate project importance and status to broader organization
- Participate in key decision gates and approvals

### Goals
- Ensure project delivers strategic business value
- Minimize delays due to organizational blockers
- Maximize stakeholder confidence and transparency
- Enable quick decision-making on trade-offs and scope changes
- Align project outcomes with business strategy

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Receive regular status updates from **Project Managers** on progress, risks, and milestones
- Align with **Product Managers** on success metrics and business outcomes
- Empower the delivery team by removing organizational and business blockers
- Approve major scope changes, timeline adjustments, or budget modifications
- Participate in key decision gates (Initiation approval, Release approval, Go/No-go decisions)
- Escalate strategic concerns or resource needs as appropriate

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and steering committee meetings
- Approval of project charter, business case, and release plans
- Escalation and decision requests from the Project Manager
- Post-release business metrics review and ROI validation
- Budget and resource allocation discussions
- Executive communications and organizational alignment

---

## Scrum Master / Delivery Coordinator

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Delivery Coordinators facilitate project ceremonies, remove process impediments, and coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement. They protect the team's focus and maximize flow and productivity.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove process blockers and impediments
- Coach team on agile practices, ceremonies, and rituals
- Maintain project board clarity and artifact organization
- Track action items and retrospective follow-ups
- Promote team health, psychological safety, and collaboration
- Support continuous improvement and process optimization

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and delivery flow
- Minimize context switching and distractions
- Build team capability in agile practices
- Foster continuous improvement and learning culture
- Improve team collaboration and psychological safety

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Support **Developers** in sprint planning and removing technical/process blockers
- Coordinate with **Project Manager** on ceremony scheduling, risk escalation, and status reporting
- Work with **Product Manager** on backlog clarity, prioritization, and refinement sessions
- Facilitate communication across all roles and stakeholders
- Escalate impediments to **Project Manager** and **Stakeholders** as needed
- Support **QA/Testing Lead** in incorporating quality gates into ceremonies
- Assist **Technical Leads** in addressing technical blockers during standups

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies (facilitation)
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking
- Impediment escalations to PM with context and impact
- Process improvement recommendations and retro outcomes
- Team health observations and collaboration feedback
- Backlog refinement session facilitation

## Security / Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security and Compliance Officers ensure that projects meet security, privacy, and compliance requirements. They embed security early in design and throughout execution, reducing risk and ensuring regulatory adherence.

### Responsibilities
- Define security and compliance requirements for the project
- Review and approve security architecture and design
- Conduct or oversee security scanning, assessments, and penetration testing
- Define incident response and breach notification procedures
- Track compliance with applicable regulations (GDPR, SOC2, HIPAA, etc.)
- Participate in release approval and post-incident reviews
- Advise on security tooling, frameworks, and best practices

### Goals
- Ensure zero critical security vulnerabilities in production
- Maintain compliance with applicable regulations and standards
- Build secure-by-design practices across the organization
- Reduce security incidents and breach risk
- Enable fast, secure delivery without sacrificing velocity

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Partner with **Technical Leads** on secure design, architecture review, and threat modeling
- Collaborate with **Developers** on secure coding practices and vulnerability remediation
- Support **QA/Testing Lead** on security testing, penetration testing, and vulnerability assessment
- Advise **Project Managers** on security risks, compliance milestones, and mitigation timelines
- Escalate security incidents to **Stakeholders** and execute incident response procedures
- Review acceptance criteria with **Product Managers** for security and privacy requirements
- Work with **Scrum Master** on security process integration and compliance ceremonies

### Typical Communication
- Security and compliance requirements definition (in project initiation)
- Design review participation for security-critical features
- CI/CD security scanning results and vulnerability remediation status tracking
- Incident response activation and communication
- Compliance audit participation and regulatory reporting
- Security training and awareness recommendations
- Threat model review and security architecture assessments
